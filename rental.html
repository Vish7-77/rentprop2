What is a Rent Agreement?

A rent agreement is a written contract between the property owner (the landlord) and the tenant who rents the property. It also contains the terms and conditions under which the property is rented, such as its description (location, kind, and size), monthly rent, security deposit, property's usage (residential or commercial), and the agreement's duration. Terms and conditions can be discussed beforehand, but the landlord and the tenant are bound by them once signed. It also lays forth the terms under which the contract can be cancelled.

The lease agreement form, as a legal document, is highly vital. The rent agreement online protects the rights of both parties and prevents future disagreements. During the lease term, there may be various disagreements between the landlord and the tenant. Such disputes could be tackled well through a contract. It offers the landlord's property security and protects tenants against unreasonable demands.

The rental agreement document also provides the tenant with the ownership of the property for a set length of time. As we all know, verbal agreements are not enforceable by law, and it is usually a good idea to sign a written contract. Another crucial aspect is that it serves as a piece of legal evidence that can be presented for several identity proofs.

Rent agreements can be set for whatever time the parties desire or agree. It can be renewed according to the conditions of the agreement. The rent agreement’s length is usually 11 months, which is to get over the rigorous rental rule which applies to lease agreements of up to 12 months. Also, an important point to remember is that, according to the Registration Act 1908, any property leased to a tenant for more than 11 months must be registered. Hence, a property on the agreement for a few months or less than 11 months does not require registration.

Types of Rent Agreements

When a property is rented, be it commercial or residential, several legal documents such as a rent agreement, lease agreement form, or licencing agreement define the relationship between the landlord and the tenant to curb unnecessary disputes in the future.

So, what does an agreement mean? A lease agreement form is a legal document that spells out the terms and circumstances of a lease or rent partnership between two people, i.e. (the owner and the tenant). There are different agreements for renting or leasing a property, as stated earlier. The rental agreement document comes under the Rent Control Act, and the Leave and License agreement comes under the Indian Easement Act.

An agreement consists of some basic property details, the details of the owner and the tenant, the term of the agreement, and the amount to be paid as rent. The three types of rental agreements are as follows:

Rent Agreement

A rent agreement is a written contract between the property owner (the landlord) and the tenant who rents the property. It contains the terms and conditions under which the property is rented, including its description (location, kind, and size), monthly rent, security deposit, usage (residential or commercial), and agreement duration.

The rent agreement can be acquired for a shorter period of only 11 months. If the agreement exceeds the time of 11 months, then it is necessary to get a registered agreement.

The Transfer of Property Act, Specific Relief Act, Evidence Act, and Registration Act, among others, are significant legislation that governs the tenancy agreement, and one should be thorough with all of these to be on secure ground.

Lease Agreement

A lease agreement is usually for a more extended time than a rent agreement. A lease agreement form is a contract between the landlord and the tenant that grants the tenant the right to use the property for a specified period of time (generally more than 12 months).

This agreement binds the landlord and the tenant for the agreed-upon duration, from 30 to 99 years.

A lease also generates an interest in the transferable property and creates rights that can be inherited.

The lease agreement assures that the lessee does not evict the premises unless it is indicated explicitly in the agreement. A legal notice might also be served on the lessee. To be valid in a court of law, a lease deed must be stamped and registered (in case of disputes).

According to tenancy law, the person who receives the rent is the landlord. In a dispute, the person allocated to receive the rent has the authority to take all legal steps necessary to have the agreement either precisely performed or the property removed by instituting appropriate legal actions in a court of law.

Leave and License Agreement

A Leave and License Agreement is a legal instrument that allows the licence holder to use the Licensor's property. However, the property is granted under a Leave and License agreement rather than a tenancy arrangement. Therefore, the property right would be invalid without it.

The leave and license agreement come under the Indian Easement Act 1882, and it is not similar to the lease or rent agreement.

What is the Stamp Duty on Rent Agreement?

Stamp duty is a tax levied by the government on various property transactions. You must pay stamp duty when you buy a home or sign a rental agreement. Section 3 of the Indian Stamp Act, 1899, mandates the payment of stamp duty.

What is E-Stamping in the Rental Agreement?

E-stamp paper or E-stamping is a method of electronically paying stamp duty to the government. The government has replaced the conventional paper and franklin stamping approach with a digital plan to ensure a quick and trouble-free payment process. This service is currently available in 22 Indian states.
